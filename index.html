About Me

I've always been a person who does things that rewards curiosity. Space, celestial phenomena, questions that don't have neat answers yet. Data pulled me in the same way. I'm always amazed at how much meaning you can extract from the right data if you're willing to look hard enough. These instincts are what pulled me into AI and data science.

That same drive to dig deeper is what pushed me past analysis into building. I'm completing my Master's in AI and Business Analytics at USF (May 2026, GPA 3.9), and over the past two years I've shipped systems that go well beyond the notebook. Real-time promo abuse detection on Kafka and Spark, end-to-end ML pipelines on Airflow and BigQuery, and a causal uplift model that translated into a modeled $240K annualized impact. I care about the full path: from raw, messy data to a decision someone can actually act on.

Teaching AI and Analytics as a Teaching Assistant at USF has sharpened that instinct further. Explaining why a model makes a tradeoff forces you to understand it at a level that purely technical work doesn't. It's made me a sharper thinker and a more deliberate collaborator.

I'm actively looking for full-time roles in data science, AI/ML engineering, and analytics. If something here resonates, you know where to find me.
