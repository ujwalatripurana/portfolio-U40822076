About Me

I've always been a person who does things that rewards curiosity. Space, celestial phenomena, questions that don't have neat answers yet. Data pulled me in the same way. I'm always amazed at how much meaning you can extract from the right data if you're willing to look hard enough. These instincts are what pulled me into AI and data science.

That same drive to dig deeper is what pushed me past analysis into building. I'm completing my Master's in AI and Business Analytics at USF (May 2026, GPA 3.9), and over the past two years I've shipped systems that go well beyond the notebook. Real-time promo abuse detection on Kafka and Spark, end-to-end ML pipelines on Airflow and BigQuery, and a causal uplift model that translated into a modeled $240K annualized impact. I care about the full path: from raw, messy data to a decision someone can actually act on.

Teaching AI and Analytics as a Teaching Assistant at USF has sharpened that instinct further. Explaining why a model makes a tradeoff forces you to understand it at a level that purely technical work doesn't. It's made me a sharper thinker and a more deliberate collaborator.

I'm actively looking for full-time roles in data science, AI/ML engineering, and analytics. If something here resonates, you know where to find me.

Experience

My first real experience was at Omdena, where I joined a global team working on something that felt genuinely meaningful. We were predicting treatment costs for lung cancer patients so they could actually plan ahead. I cleaned messy healthcare datasets, built and validated predictive models that hit R² of 0.86, and translated everything into Power BI dashboards that clients could understand without a data background. It was the first time I saw the full arc from raw data to a decision that mattered to someone.

From there I moved into marketing analytics at ANS Commerce, managing paid campaigns for multiple brands at over $3,000 in daily spend. The job was equal parts analysis and instinct. I built ETL pipelines to clean the data, run A/B tests to find what worked, dug into root-cause analysis when numbers dipped. I kept ROI between 5x and 6x, CPA at $12, and CTR at 3.6% across campaigns. It taught me how to move fast with data and defend every number.

Now I teach AI and Analytics as a Graduate Teaching Assistant at USF while finishing my Master's. Helping students debug models and reason through tradeoffs has made me a sharper thinker than any purely technical project has. It also keeps me close to the fundamentals at a time when everything in the field is moving fast.
